Project Summary

For our project, we visualized the percentage of citizens without a high school diploma with average incomes across Washington counties. Free public education is an integral and valuable part of the United States community, and the opportunity to attend school prepares citizens to potentially continue in higher education or enter the professional world. There is a clear correlation between higher average incomes and higher rates of high school graduation, so it is paramount that United States citizens know the importance of graduating high school. To represent income we used proportional symbols on top of a choropleth background for high school graduation rates.

Data

We sourced income data from the US Census , and graduation data from from the Washington Geospatial Open Data Portal The income data was manipulated using Excel to organize each GEOID to ensure it could be joined with a shapefile. The graduation data had the field 'Percentage_Without_Diploma' stored as a string rather than a double, so we had to convert that attribute. Additionally, the data was polygon rather than point, so we used QGIS to find centroids and map that point coordinate instead.
